Puff Puff

Description

A very popular West African street food that’s quick and easy to make with different variations. Dangerously delicious and addicting!

Ingredients

  • flour
  • yeast
  • warm water
  • salt
  • sugar
  • cooking oil

STEPS

  1. Rest and Rise: Let the mixture rise for approximately 1-2 hours (See photos 3-5)
  2. Pre-heat the oil: In a large sauce pan, pour vegetable oil into a pot until it’s about 3 inches (or 5 centimeters ) high (too little will result into a flatter balls), and place in low heat
  3. Test to make sure the oil is hot enough by putting a drop of batter into the oil. If the oil is not hot enough, the batter will stay at the bottom instead of rising to the top.
  4. Hand Method: Using your hands, grab a little bit of mixture at a time and drop into the oil. (See photo 6) Spoon Method: When the oil is hot enough, use a spoon to dish up the batter and another spoon or spatula to drop it in the oil. Sort of in the shape of a ball.
  5. Check the doneness: Fry for a few minutes until the bottom side is golden brown
  6. Use a large spoon or a slotted spoon to take it out of the oil. I usually place them on napkins right away to soak up some of the excess oil.
  7. If desired, you can roll up the finished products in table sugar or powdered sugar to make it sweeter. There you have it. Delicious puff puff!
